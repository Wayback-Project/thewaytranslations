# Strategy: Lamsa-Oriented Aramaic Primacy Framework (Top 50 Focus Areas)

Created: 2026-04-08 UTC  
Status: Working strategy draft for The Way One

## Core project rules (editor confirmed)

1. The Way One follows **Aramaic primacy** as the lead restoration posture.
2. We prioritize the **spoken-world texture** of the text, not only later institutional renderings.
3. **Hard rule: do not remove text.** Keep all books/verses in corpus.
4. Where Lamsa/Peshitta has shorter canon or differs, preserve those differences in notes, not by deleting material.
5. For later writings with strong Greek textual grounding (for example, Pauline letters), acknowledge the shift and treat Greek evidence as primary there.

---

## Operational track model

- **Track A (Aramaic-priority track):** Gospels + core Jesus sayings + Semitic idiom-dense passages.
- **Track B (Greek-priority acknowledged track):** Later apostolic letters and books with clear Greek manuscript primacy.
- **Track C (Dual witness track):** Passages where Aramaic and Greek diverge strongly; show side-by-side rationale.

## Top 50 focus areas to normalize in translation workflow

2. No-removal rule for all canonical text in project corpus.
3. Mark books absent in early Peshitta as "later inclusion" notes, not deletions.
4. Distinguish textual base vs interpretive lens in every major decision.
5. Maintain variant ledger (Aramaic reading / Greek reading / chosen rendering / reason).

7. Add explicit book-level source priority tags (A/B/C track).
8. Require rationale note for any verse with major wording divergence.

### C. Spoken-language and idiom restoration (36-42)
